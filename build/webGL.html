Why Use WebGL?

This page demonstrates 3D graphics using WebGL. The idea is to combine WebGL with live data to visually convey the state of the SPELUNCR rover. For example, the orientation of the rover and the positions/orientations of components on the rover (think steering, solar arrays, robotic arms, etc.) can be shown in real time with a 3D model. Additionally, the shading of components on the WebGL model can reflect the conditions of specific components on the rover. Examples of this could include shading healthy components blue and unhealthy components red (to indicate problems like a flat tire or a hot battery) or placing green dots on the locations of each sensor that initialize successfully when the rover is started. Many other visualizations can be possible using WebGL.
